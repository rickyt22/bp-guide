CMS Reimbursement
RPM CPT Codes & Revenue Potential

Medicare reimburses eligible RPM services through four CPT codes. Your clinic bills directly — 22RPM provides the platform, devices, and documentation to do it right.

99453
~$19
One-Time · Per Enrollment
Initial device setup and patient education. Billed once per patient at enrollment.
99454
~$48/mo
Monthly · Requires 16+ Days
Device supply with daily recording. Minimum 16 days of data per 30-day period required.
99457
~$50/mo
Monthly · First 20 Minutes
Remote monitoring care management — first 20 minutes per calendar month.
99458
~$41/mo
Monthly · Each Add’l 20 Min
Each additional 20-minute increment. Can be billed multiple times per month.

Estimated Monthly Revenue Per Enrolled Patient

~$139+

Billing 99454 + 99457 per compliant patient. A practice with 50 active RPM patients may generate an estimated $6,500–$8,000/month in additional reimbursement.
Rates are 2024 national Medicare averages. Actual amounts vary by geography and payer mix.

Compliance note: Your clinic bills CMS directly and retains all reimbursements. 22RPM charges a flat monthly service fee — not a percentage of Medicare reimbursements. This structure supports Anti-Kickback Statute compliance.

Common Questions
Provider FAQ

Everything your practice needs to know before launching RPM.

Is RPM the same as telehealth?
+
No. RPM is a separate service category with monthly recurring reimbursements — not encounter-based like telehealth. RPM and telehealth can be billed together for the same patient in the same month.
What are the patient qualifications for RPM?
+
Patients must have at least one chronic condition and be enrolled in Medicare Part B. The ordering provider must document medical necessity and patients must provide consent before devices are deployed. 22RPM helps identify eligible patients from your existing panel.
Do I need to hire monitoring staff?
+
No additional staff is required to launch. The 22RPM platform delivers real-time data and threshold alerts to your existing team. Your providers log care management time for 99457/99458 — typically a brief monthly check-in with the patient.
How many days of data does a patient need to transmit?
+
CMS requires at least 16 days of data transmission in a 30-day period to bill CPT 99454. The 22RPM dashboard tracks each patient’s transmission days in real time so your team always knows where every patient stands before the billing cycle closes.
Who handles the billing — 22RPM or our clinic?
+
Your clinic bills CMS directly using your provider NPI. 22RPM provides the timestamped reading logs, transmission day counts, and documentation your billing team needs. We do not bill on your behalf and do not take a percentage of reimbursements.
Is there an out-of-pocket cost for patients?
+
Under traditional Medicare, patients are generally responsible for standard 20% co-insurance. Many patients with Medigap supplemental plans have this fully covered. We recommend confirming with your billing team for each patient’s specific plan.
How secure is patient data?
+
22RPM’s platform is built on AWS with a signed BAA. All patient data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS/HTTPS). Access requires authenticated login with OTP verification. AWS CloudTrail provides complete audit logging.
How long does it take to launch?
+
Most clinics are fully operational within one week of signing. Day 1–2: agreements signed. Day 2–4: patient panel reviewed. Day 4–7: devices shipped. Week 2: first readings live. Month 1: first billing cycle complete with full documentation.
Can a patient be enrolled in RPM and another program simultaneously?
+
Yes, with some restrictions. A patient can be enrolled in both RPM (99454) and Chronic Care Management (CCM) simultaneously. However, a patient cannot be enrolled in RPM under two different providers at the same time.
What types of insurance cover RPM?
+
Medicare Part B covers RPM for eligible patients with chronic conditions. Many Medicaid programs and commercial insurance plans also cover RPM, though coverage varies by state and plan. Verify coverage with your billing team prior to enrollment.
