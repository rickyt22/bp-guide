How It Works
Four Simple Steps. That Is All.

From the day your device arrives to the moment your care team sees your reading.

1

Device Setup & Getting Started

After your doctor enrolls you, our team will get your device set up and ready to use. You’ll receive everything you need to begin monitoring your health right away.

💡 Our team walks you through setup so there is nothing to figure out on your own.
2

Download the App & Log In

Download the free 22RPM app from the App Store. Your care team gives you a username and password. Log in, enter the short code from your email, and you are ready.

💡 After first login, set up Face ID so you never have to type your password again.
3

Take Your Reading — It Sends Automatically

Sit quietly for 5 minutes, put on the cuff, and press Start. The device does the rest. Your reading appears in the app and goes directly to your care team automatically.

💡 Most doctors recommend once in the morning before medications and once in the evening.
4

Your Care Team Monitors Your Progress

Your doctor and care team see all your readings in real time. If something looks unusual, they will reach out to you. You can also view your own history in the app anytime.

🚨 If your reading is ever over 180/120 and you feel unwell, call 911 right away.

Know Your Numbers
Understanding Your Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is measured as two numbers — for example, 120/78. The top number (systolic) measures pressure when your heart beats. The bottom (diastolic) measures pressure between beats. Categories below are based on American Heart Association guidelines.

CategoryTop NumberBottom NumberWhat To Do
NormalLess than 120Less than 80Keep taking readings as scheduled. Keep up the great work!
Elevated120–129Less than 80Your care team is monitoring. Lifestyle changes may help.
High Blood Pressure
Stage 1		130–13980–89Your care team may reach out. Medication or lifestyle changes may be recommended.
High Blood Pressure
Stage 2		140 or higher90 or higherContact your doctor promptly if you have not heard from them.
Hypertensive CrisisHigher than 180Higher than 120Seek emergency care immediately. Call 911.
🚨
If your blood pressure reads higher than 180/120 AND you experience a severe headache, chest pain, difficulty breathing, numbness, or vision changes — call 911 immediately. This is a medical emergency.

Blood pressure categories based on American Heart Association guidelines. Always follow your doctor’s specific guidance.

Common Questions
Patient FAQ

Common questions from patients just like you — answered simply.

Will this cost me anything?
+
RPM is a Medicare benefit. Under traditional Medicare you may be responsible for standard 20% co-insurance. Many patients with Medigap supplemental coverage have this fully covered. 22RPM does not charge patients directly.
What if I forget to take my reading?
+
That is okay — just take it the next day as usual. Medicare requires at least 16 readings per 30-day period. If you are having trouble staying consistent, let your care team know and they can help you build a daily routine that works.
Is my personal information safe?
+
Yes. Your health data is encrypted and stored securely on AWS infrastructure. Only your authorized care team can see your readings. 22RPM does not sell, share, or use your data for advertising. A formal HIPAA Business Associate Agreement protects your information.
What if my reading looks very high or low?
+
Take the reading again after resting quietly for 2–3 minutes with the cuff on bare skin. If readings remain very high (over 180/120) and you feel unwell, call 911. Your care team monitors your readings and will contact you if they see anything concerning.
Do I need Wi-Fi at home?
+
You need either Wi-Fi or a cell signal on your phone for readings to reach your care team. If you take a reading without connectivity, the app stores it and sends automatically the next time you are connected.
What if I am not comfortable with technology?
+
We designed this program specifically for patients who are new to technology. The setup guide has large text, real screenshots, and is in English and Spanish. Email us at [email protected] and we will walk you through every step together.
Who can see my readings?
+
Only your authorized care team. 22RPM staff can access data for technical support only and are bound by HIPAA. Nobody else has access to your readings.
How long will I be in the program?
+
As long as your doctor determines it is medically appropriate. Many patients remain enrolled for months or years while managing chronic conditions. Your doctor will review your program periodically.
